Open Education

For centuries learning has been restricted to institutions and academia. It often costed thousands of dollars, was very competitive, and open only to a select few. The internet opens up the availability of information and thus has allowed anyone and everyone to have access to the best courses, professors, and lectures. It has also allowed interactive game-based learning that can be more engaging that sitting through a lecture.

Coursera

Coursera partners with the top universities in the world to offer courses online for anyone to take, for free. They envision a future where the top universities are educating not only thousands of students, but millions. Their technology enables the best professors to teach tens or hundreds of thousands of students.

Visit Site »

Khan Academy

Learn almost anything for free. With a library of over 3,500 videos on everything from arithmetic to physics, finance, and history and hundreds of skills to practice, Khan will help you learn what you want, when you want, at your own pace. Khan differs in that he talks you step by step through each concept, showing you the blackboard, not the teacher.

Visit Site »

Udacity

Udacity believes university-level education can be both high quality and low cost. Using the economics of the Internet, they've connected some of the greatest teachers to hundreds of thousands of students in almost every country on Earth. Udacity was founded by three roboticists, including Sebastian Thrun of Stanford's winning (now Google) self-driving cars and Google Glass.

Visit Site »

edX

EdX is a not-for-profit founded by Harvard and MIT that features learning designed specifically for interactive study via the web. They are creating a new experience with courses that reflect their disciplinary breadth. The institutions will also use edX to research how students learn and how technology can transform learning–both on-campus and worldwide.

Visit Site »

iTunes U

iTunes U is Apple's shot at transforming education through technology. They offer programs that let any teacher easily create great courses and content, and an iPad app that interacts with rich dynamic textbooks and organizes your notes, your homework, and class materials. You can also access iTunes U through iTunes and browse thousands of lectures from around the world.

Visit Site »

Open Culture

Open Culture brings together high-quality cultural & educational media. Web 2.0 has given us lots of intelligent audio and video that's free, enriching, but it’s also scattered across the web. Their mission is to centralize this content, curate it, and give you access to it. They offer free audio books, free online courses, free movies, free language lessons, free ebooks and other enriching content.

Visit Site »

Cosmo Learning

Created with the goal to provide a free online school, CosmoLearning (CL) is an educational website committed to improve the quality of homeschooling, teaching and student excellence, helping educators and self-learners alike anywhere in the world. Designed to work as a free homeschool and organized according to traditional curriculum standards, CosmoLearning provides video lectures, courses, documentaries, books, quizzes, lecture notes and much more.

Visit Site »

Academic Earth

Academic Earth is an organization founded with the goal of giving everyone on earth access to a world-class education, by connecting you to top universities and scholars. As more and more high quality free educational content comes online, they ask: what are the real barriers to achieving a world class education?

Visit Site »

Udemy

Udemy helps students make moves. Whether you want to get promoted, break into a new industry, start a company, further a passion, or just accelerate your life, Udemy helps you learn from the amazing instructors in the world, so that you can get there and get there faster. Their team recruits the world's top experts, including New York Times best-selling authors, CEOs, celebrities, and Ivy League professors.

Visit Site »

TED

TED stands for Technology, Entertainment, and Design. TED has a history of featuring the most riveting talks from scientists, artists, politicans, creators, basically anyone that has something meaningful to say. With the tagline of 'ideas worth spreading' every talk is limited to ~20 mins, and this limit often forces people to get to the real nugget of the 'idea worth spreading'.

Visit Site »

Fora.tv

At FORA.tv, they believe that there are brilliant ideas expressed everyday, everywhere, and they don't want you to miss them. They've gathered the web's largest collection of unmediated video drawn from top conferences, universities, and public forums. They present this provocative, big-idea content for you to watch, interact with, and share -when, where, and how you want.

Visit Site »

RSA Animate

The RSA (Royal Society for the encouragement of Arts, Manufactures and Commerce): an enlightenment organisation committed to finding innovative practical solutions to today’s social challenges. I believe it's a conference held once a year with speakers similar to TED. They then condense these ideas into 5 minute drawing to help you follow along, it's bloody brilliant!

Visit Site »

Ignite

If you thought TED was sacrificing brevity for substance, then walk away. If, on the other hand, you're used to 2 minute internet videos...Ignite is a geek event in over 100 global cities. Presenters share their personal and professional passions, using 20 slides that auto-advance every 15 seconds for a total of just five minutes.

Visit Site »

Big Think

In our digital age, we’re drowning in information. The web offers us infinite data points—news stories, tweets, wikis, status updates, etc—but very little to connect the dots or illuminate the larger patterns linking them together.
